# Customer Churn Analysis

## Project Overview

Customer churn is one of the biggest challenges faced by telecom companies. When customers leave a service provider, the company loses revenue and must spend additional resources to acquire new customers. Understanding why customers leave is therefore important for improving customer retention and business performance.

In this project, I performed Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on a telecom customer dataset to identify the factors that contribute to customer churn. The analysis focuses on customer demographics, service subscriptions, account information, and contract details to uncover meaningful patterns and insights.

---

## Business Problem

The telecom company wants to understand:

* Which customers are more likely to churn?
* What factors influence customer retention?
* Which services are associated with higher or lower churn rates?
* How can the company reduce customer attrition and improve customer satisfaction?

The goal of this project is to analyze customer behavior and provide data-driven recommendations that can help the company retain more customers.

## Key Insights

### 1. Contract Type has a Strong Impact on Churn

Customers with month-to-month contracts have the highest churn rate. Customers with one-year and two-year contracts are much more likely to stay with the company.

### 2. Fiber Optic Customers Churn More Frequently

Customers using fiber optic internet services show significantly higher churn compared to DSL users and customers without internet service.

### 3. Online Security Reduces Churn

Customers who subscribe to online security services are less likely to leave the company.

### 4. Tech Support Improves Customer Retention

Customers without tech support exhibit higher churn rates, highlighting the importance of customer assistance services.

### 5. Device Protection and Online Backup Matter

Customers who do not use device protection or online backup services are more likely to churn.

### 6. Family-Oriented Customers Stay Longer

Customers with partners and dependents tend to remain with the company longer than those without them.

### 7. Tenure is Related to Customer Loyalty

Customers with longer tenure generally have lower churn rates than newly acquired customers.

---

## Business Recommendations

### 1. Promote Long-Term Contracts

Offer discounts, loyalty rewards, and special benefits to encourage customers to switch from month-to-month plans to annual contracts.

### 2. Improve Fiber Optic Customer Experience

Investigate service quality, pricing, and customer satisfaction issues related to fiber optic internet users.

### 3. Bundle Value-Added Services

Include Online Security, Online Backup, Device Protection, and Tech Support in service packages to improve retention.

### 4. Focus on New Customers

Develop onboarding programs and personalized support for customers during their initial months of service.

### 5. Implement Churn Monitoring

Use predictive analytics and customer behavior monitoring to identify high-risk customers before they leave.

### 6. Strengthen Customer Engagement

Create loyalty programs, exclusive offers, and personalized communication strategies to improve customer satisfaction.

---

## Conclusion

This analysis shows that contract type, internet service type, and value-added support services are among the most important factors influencing customer churn. By focusing on customer retention strategies and improving service quality, telecom companies can reduce churn, improve customer satisfaction, and increase long-term profitability.
